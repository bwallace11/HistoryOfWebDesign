History of HTML

An educational website documenting the evolution of HyperText Markup Language from its creation at CERN to the modern living standard.

Introduction

HTML's history spans from Tim Berners-Lee's 1991 CERN proposal through HTML5's 2014 standardization to today's Living Standard model, evolving through three distinct eras: the Early Web (1990–1995) establishing foundational semantics, the Browser Wars (1995–2001) fragmenting under commercial competition, and the Modern Web (2004–present) unifying around HTML5's pragmatic platform vision.

This site focuses on how HTML evolved, who shaped it, and why modern HTML is maintained as a living standard.
