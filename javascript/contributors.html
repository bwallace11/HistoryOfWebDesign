Major Contributors to JavaScript

Brendan Eich

Created JavaScript in May 1995 while working at Netscape Communications in approximately ten days. Later co-founded Mozilla and served as its CEO.

Allen Wirfs-Brock

Lead editor of ECMAScript 5 (2009) and ECMAScript 2015 (ES6). A central figure in rebuilding the standards process after the collapse of ES4. Co-authored JavaScript: The First 20 Years with Brendan Eich.

Waldemar Horwat

Long-standing contributor to ECMAScript and TC39, with involvement dating to the earliest Netscape-era standardization efforts. Has served as editor of multiple ECMAScript editions.

Ryan Dahl

Created Node.js in 2009, enabling JavaScript to run outside the browser as a server-side runtime. Later created Deno (2018) as a more secure alternative.

Douglas Crockford

Popularized JSON as a lightweight data-interchange format and authored JavaScript: The Good Parts (2008), arguing for a disciplined subset of the language.

TC39 Committee

Technical Committee 39 of Ecma International is the collective body responsible for the ongoing evolution of ECMAScript. Members include delegates from browser vendors and large companies. All specification changes go through TC39's staged proposal process.
